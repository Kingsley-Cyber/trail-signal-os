# Mission and Research Thesis

## Mission

Create a continuously expandable research corpus that identifies overlooked product opportunities hidden inside common outdoor behavior.

## Thesis

Large product categories are often too broad to reveal opportunity. Useful opportunities appear at the interface between a recurring task and a constraint:

`ordinary participant + repeated action + environmental constraint + failed interface`

Examples include retrieving tackle with wet hands, carrying dog-walking objects with one hand occupied, moving through a full running stride without garment restriction, or setting up camp lighting while managing children.

## Unit of analysis

The unit is not “fishing,” “running,” or “camping.” It is an atomic task episode:

- actor and experience level;
- task and desired outcome;
- body position and occupied hands;
- environment and season;
- tools already in use;
- failure or inconvenience;
- improvised workaround;
- measurable improvement.

## Research objective

Find a narrow product interface whose value can be understood in one sentence and tested before meaningful inventory commitment.

## Exclusions

The first-pass system deprioritizes products whose failure can directly cause death or severe injury, regulated weapons, fuel, ingestibles, medical claims, counterfeit-adjacent products, products requiring unverifiable certifications, and products likely to produce high fit-related returns.
